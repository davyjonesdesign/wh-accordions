Many states require vehicles to pass inspections every one to two years before registration renewal. These inspections may include safety checks, smog tests, or VIN verification. This ensures that your car is safe to drive and doesn’t harm the environment. Issues like a cracked windshield, worn brakes, or a leaky exhaust can make a vehicle unsafe or contribute to air pollution. By requiring these checks, states aim to keep hazardous vehicles off the road and protect public safety.
